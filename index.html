Welcome to Ms. Love's Classroom!

This is a guide to everything you need to know for Ms. Love's class and 10th grade in general!

English Tips Sophomore Tips

  • Tip 1: Memorize your roots and lexicon words! ü§î

    Create flashcards or a Quizlet for each of the greek/latin roots and lexicon words every week, and review them in your downtime to ensure you have them memorized!

  • Tip 2: Have a good knowledge of history! üè∞

    By having a basic understanding of history, including Victorian England and World War II, you can have a deeper understanding of the nuance of certain books.

  • Tip 3: Pace your reading and stick to it üìö

    Especially for lit circle books, make sure you actually reach the reading goals you set for yourself and don't get behind. If you fall behind on your reading, it's easy to keep falling more behind, hurting your understanding and ability to do well on assignments.

  • Tip 4: Memorize your analytical writing verbs!

    Create flashcards or a Quizlet for the analytical writing verbs you get at the beginning of the school year, and then review them before on-demand writing prompts. The analytical writing verbs work to make your writing stronger and improves your grades.

  • Tip 5: Understand Literary Devices

    Make sure you have a basic understanding of some of the basic literary devices (metaphors, similes, imagery, characterization, jutxaposition) as you will be asked to analyze them in your writing prompts. By having a grasp on those concepts, you can better analyze your texts and perform better on your writing assignments.

  • Tip 6: Think critically about the texts you read

    Try to understand the different aspects of different characters in a novel or why authors make certain stylistic or narrative choices. Considering these allows you to understand more about your book and improves your comprehension for writing prompts.

  • Tip 7: Be confident and don't fear public speaking

    Being in Honors 10th grade English means having to do a number of assignments that require you to address your class or audiences of people. Although this may seem scary and daunting at first, try not to get the anxiety or stage fright get the most of you, and present as confidently and clearly as you can.

  • Tip 8: Discuss your novel

    Try to discuss your books with your friends or classmates as much as possible. This helps you gain more perspective on aspects of the book and have a better understanding of the work overall.

  • Tip 9: For your persuasive project, be organized

    When working on your persuasive project, try to be as organized as possible, keeping track of all of your sources, messages and tasks. This will help you when you're assembling your final presentations and binder.

  • Tip 10: Pay attention to feedback

    The writing feedback on on-demand assignments can be very helpful in allowing you to improve your writing. Use the feedback key Ms. Love provides and be sure to ask if you need clarification or extra detail on your score.
